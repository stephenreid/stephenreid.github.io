Hackathons and Random Hacks of Kindness

I've really enjoyed playing around with applications and meeting people at Atlanta Hackathons (which really took off). They're a great way to learn about new technologies, write some code, and enjoy thinking about great ideas. As the API Evangelist at Pardot, I actually helped Georgia Tech Young Entrepreneur's Society pull off some and advised some to how to use Pardot.

Random Hacks of Kindness and now Gov/City Hack have been great ways to encourage involvement with the city of Atlanta. At my first one, we worked with mesh networking and eventual consistency on the iPhone / Bluetooth stack with a project that we call MessageCarrier. At my second RHOK, we helped somebody from the CDC help track polio outbreaks.
